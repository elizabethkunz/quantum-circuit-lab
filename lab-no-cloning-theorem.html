Lab · No Cloning and BB84

Can an eavesdropper copy a quantum message without being detected?

In BB84, Alice sends random qubits prepared in one of two incompatible bases. Bob measures each qubit in a random basis and later compares basis choices with Alice over a public channel.

If Eve intercepts and measures qubits to copy the message, she must guess a basis. Wrong guesses disturb the state. That disturbance appears as extra errors in Alice and Bob's sifted key.

Task 02

Interpret the no-cloning limit.

BB84 security comes from a structural fact about quantum mechanics, not from Eve lacking compute power.

No-cloning theorem

There is no universal unitary operation U such that U |psi⟩|0⟩ = |psi⟩|psi⟩ for every unknown state |psi⟩.

In BB84, states from the Z and X bases are non-orthogonal as a set. Eve cannot perfectly copy an unknown incoming qubit and delay measurement until basis information is announced.

Intercept-resend forces Eve to measure immediately in a guessed basis. Wrong guesses randomize Bob outcomes in sifted bits, producing a detectable error signature.
