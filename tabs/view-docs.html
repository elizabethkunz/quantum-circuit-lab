The literature, briefly annotated.

The canonical papers, standard textbooks, and a few key online resources. Organised by theme — start anywhere.

Foundations

Feynman · 1982
Simulating Physics with Computers

The paper that started it all. Feynman argues that classical computers cannot efficiently simulate quantum systems, and that a quantum computer could. The seed of the entire field.

foundationalsimulation
Deutsch · 1985
Quantum Theory, the Church-Turing Principle and the Universal Quantum Computer

Formalises the quantum Turing machine and presents the first quantum algorithm — a simple problem solved in one query where classical computers need two.

foundationalalgorithms
Bell · 1964
On the Einstein Podolsky Rosen Paradox

Bell proves that no local hidden-variable theory can reproduce all predictions of quantum mechanics. Entanglement is not just a bookkeeping trick — it's physically real.

entanglementnonlocality
Wootters & Zurek · 1982
A Single Quantum Cannot Be Cloned

The no-cloning theorem, proved in one page. An unknown quantum state cannot be duplicated — with direct consequences for error correction, cryptography, and quantum networking.

no-cloningtheorem

Algorithms

Shor · 1994
Algorithms for Quantum Computation: Discrete Logarithms and Factoring

Exponential speedup for integer factorisation. If a fault-tolerant quantum computer is ever built at scale, RSA encryption becomes obsolete. The paper that made governments pay attention.

factoringcryptographyexponential speedup
Grover · 1996
A Fast Quantum Mechanical Algorithm for Database Search

Quadratic speedup for unstructured search: finds an item in N possibilities using only √N queries. The canonical example of amplitude amplification, now a standard subroutine in larger algorithms.

searchquadratic speedup
Harrow, Hassidim & Lloyd · 2009
Quantum Algorithm for Linear Systems of Equations (HHL)

Exponential speedup for solving sparse linear systems under certain conditions. Foundational for proposed quantum speedups in machine learning and optimisation, though the caveats are steep.

linear algebrahhl

Error correction

Shor · 1995
Scheme for Reducing Decoherence in Quantum Computer Memory

The first quantum error-correcting code. Encodes one logical qubit in nine physical qubits and corrects arbitrary single-qubit errors — proving fault-tolerant quantum computing is not in principle impossible.

qecshor code
Kitaev · 2003
Fault-Tolerant Quantum Computation by Anyons

Introduces the toric code and topological quantum error correction. The surface code — used by Google and IBM — is its planar variant. The idea: encode information in global topology, not local physical bits.

surface codetopological
Fowler et al. · 2012
Surface Codes: Towards Practical Large-Scale Quantum Computation

The definitive reference for the surface code. Covers thresholds, resource estimates, and logical gate sets. If you want to understand what IBM and Google are actually building, start here.

surface codehardwarereview
Google Quantum AI · 2023
Suppressing quantum errors by scaling a surface code logical qubit

First experimental demonstration that increasing code distance actually reduces logical error rates — the key requirement for scalable fault tolerance. A landmark result in the field's history.

experimentmilestone2023

Textbooks & courses

Nielsen & Chuang · 2000
Quantum Computation and Quantum Information

The textbook. Comprehensive, rigorous, and thorough. Universally known as "Mike and Ike." If you only read one book on quantum computing, it's this one — though the pace demands real commitment.

textbookcomprehensive
Preskill · ongoing
Lecture Notes on Quantum Computation (Ph219, Caltech)

John Preskill's course notes, freely available. Some of the clearest explanations in the field, and regularly updated. Chapter 7 on quantum error correction is particularly authoritative.

lecture notesfree
Aaronson · 2013
Quantum Computing Since Democritus

A wide-ranging and unusually readable exploration of computation, complexity, and quantum mechanics. Less formal than Nielsen & Chuang — good for building intuition and understanding the why.

accessiblecomplexity theory

Protocols & information theory

Bennett & Brassard · 1984
Quantum Cryptography: Public Key Distribution and Coin Tossing (BB84)

The first quantum key distribution protocol. Uses the no-cloning theorem to detect eavesdroppers. BB84 is now deployed in commercial quantum cryptography systems around the world.

cryptographyqkd
Bennett et al. · 1993
Teleporting an Unknown Quantum State via Dual Classical and Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen Channels

The original teleportation paper. Shows that an unknown qubit state can be transferred using a pre-shared Bell pair plus two classical bits of communication — no faster-than-light signalling required.

teleportationentanglement
Bennett & Wiesner · 1992
Communication via One- and Two-Particle Operators on Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen States

Introduces superdense coding: two classical bits transmitted by sending a single qubit, given pre-shared entanglement. The dual of teleportation, and a clean proof that entanglement has operational value.

superdense codingcommunication

Hardware milestones

Google Quantum AI · 2019
Quantum supremacy using a programmable superconducting processor

First claimed quantum advantage: a 53-qubit processor completes a sampling task in 200 seconds estimated to take 10,000 years classically. The claim is contested, but the engineering is not.

superconductingmilestonesupremacy
Aspect, Clauser, Zeilinger · 2022
Nobel Prize in Physics — experiments with entangled photons

Awarded for experimental tests of Bell inequalities, definitively ruling out local hidden variables. The physics underlying every quantum cryptography and networking system built today.

nobel prizebell inequalities
Preskill · 2018
Quantum Computing in the NISQ Era and Beyond

Coined the term NISQ (Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum). A clear-eyed assessment of where quantum computing actually is and what it might achieve in the near term — required reading for practitioners.

nisqperspectivenear-term
